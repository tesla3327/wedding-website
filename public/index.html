The Proposal Story

On Tuesday, October 13th, Michael texted Grace saying that he wanted to take her out on a date on Friday. Grace immediately knew he was going to propose, because Michael was never this prepared for dates.

On Friday evening Michael picked up Grace from Toronto, bringing rootbeer, her favourite pop, and an assortment of different Dutch treats.

He also presented to her the first set of flowers. Two white roses, representing the two months that they knew each other as “friends” before they started dating.

They set off for their destination, and as they got closer Michael realized he had punched in the wrong address. He had meant to go to Goodwood Conservation area, but instead they arrived at Greenwood Conservation area. It was getting dark and they were hungry, so they decided to stay anyways.

Grace got her second set of flowers, 8 red roses to represent the 8 months they dated while Michael was in Manitoba and Grace was in Ontario. The third set of flowers were 10 red roses to represent the 10 months they had spent dating while they were both in Ontario.

Then, in the coldness of late October, they had a picnic with wine and cheese and chocolate. Michael played a song on guitar that he had written for Grace, and even though his fingers were numb and he couldn’t play the guitar, she loved it.

Then Michael proposed to Grace. The ring was the final event of the night, representing all of the months to come in the future. And clearly she said yes!
